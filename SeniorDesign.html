Description

Our team was tasked with building a fully autonomous smart greenhouse to grow various herbs & plants.
While no formal requirements were given for the project, our group decided on the following:

  • Aspects of the greenhouse must be automated
  • Features must be easily modifiable through a user interface
  • Design must accommodate multiple different herbs/plants
  • Project must cost under $1000 in total

In addition to this, our group has decided to design & build our own testbed for implementing this project.
The testbed will consist of a wooden greenhouse with dedicated spaces for sensors & growing equipment.
While the group does not currently have a design for this, we will be working on it in the coming weeks.

Contributions/Roles

As a group, we decided domains & areas of expertise to each member. My role in the group was AGILE leader
& backend developer. Currently, our group is still in the design phase, so my contributions are limited to
backend-wireframing, & AGILE related tasks. As our leader in AGILE, I'm responsible for:

  1. Creating & assigning tasks on a sprint board
  2. Estimating workload through a point system each sprint
  3. Keeping members on track with set deadlines
  4. Managing our Gitlab repo

At this time it's considered a stretch goal by the group, however I will also be implementing data logging into a
SQL table. This could eventually be used to track growth trends in plants throughout the greenhouse. More info to
come on this as the project progresses.

Knowledge gain

Although we still haven't implemented our project, I've still learned a good bit from these few weeks.
Walking into the project I had the mindset of corporate work. During past internships I've had the environment
was very established & professional. Not to say my group isn't professional, but the modular styling isn't quite
there yet. This has caused me to realize I need to take a leadership role. Implementing a project planning
style (like AGILE) will greatly increase our group's productivity. I've also learned a bit on how to present
to a group in a way that will produce a desired outcome. For example, I created a sprint board to track our progress
& presented it to the group. I did so in a way that was accessible & easy to learn for the group. Technical knowledge
gain to come here....
